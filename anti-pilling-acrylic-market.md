<p><p>The anti-pilling acrylic market includes two primary types: pure spinning and mixed spinning. Pure spinning involves using 100% acrylic fibers, ensuring high-quality, durable fabrics that resist pilling effectively. In contrast, mixed spinning combines acrylic fibers with other materials, enhancing specific properties like softness, versatility, or cost-effectiveness. Both approaches cater to diverse consumer preferences, with pure spinning focusing on performance and durability, while mixed spinning balances quality and affordability, ultimately expanding the application range of anti-pilling acrylic in textiles.</p></p>
