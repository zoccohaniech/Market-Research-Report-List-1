<p><p>The travel sickness drugs market comprises three main types: anticholinergics, antihistamines, and other treatments. Anticholinergics, such as scopolamine, work by blocking nerve signals that cause nausea and motion sickness. Antihistamines, like diphenhydramine and dimenhydrinate, counteract the effects of histamine, reducing symptoms of travel sickness. The "others" category includes non-pharmacological options like acupressure bands and herbal remedies. Each type offers various mechanisms of action and suitability for different individuals, catering to diverse consumer needs in managing travel-related nausea.</p></p>
