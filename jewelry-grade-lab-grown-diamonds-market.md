<p><ul><li>CVD</li><li>HPHT</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The jewelry grade lab-grown diamond market primarily features two production methods: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and High-Pressure High-Temperature (HPHT). CVD diamonds are created by depositing carbon gases in a controlled environment, resulting in high-quality stones with fewer impurities. Conversely, HPHT mimics natural diamond formation by applying high pressure and temperature to carbon, producing diamonds with unique characteristics. Both techniques yield diamonds that are chemically and structurally identical to natural diamonds, catering to a growing demand for ethical and sustainable alternatives.</p></p>
