<p><p>The preventive vaccines market consists of various administration types, primarily intramuscular injections, nasal sprays, and intradermal shots. Intramuscular injections deliver vaccines deep into the muscle, ensuring robust immune response, commonly used for most vaccines. Nasal sprays provide a needle-free alternative, offering ease of administration, particularly desirable for children. Intradermal shots inject the vaccine just below the skin's surface, allowing for a targeted immune response and reduced dosage, making it a cost-effective option in certain cases.</p></p>
